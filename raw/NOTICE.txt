The signing keys provided here are only used to verify authenticity of packages made my ZorinOS.
They are technical data only and therefore cannot be copyrighted or DMCA struck.

Copyright requires originality (a minimal creative authorship), and facts/data generated by rules aren’t protected as expressive works.
The Supreme Court held that facts are not copyrightable and only original expression/selection/arrangement can be.
cit: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/499/340/ (https://web.archive.org/web/20250826001518/https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/499/340/)

A GPG key is essentially functional data (numbers/parameters used by a cryptographic system),
and copyright does not extend to “any idea, procedure, process, system, [or] method of operation.”
cit: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/102 (https://web.archive.org/web/20251212035256/https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/102)

Courts have rejected copyright in identifier-like strings produced by a rigid system (e.g., part numbers), because they lack originality.
That reasoning maps well onto cryptographic key material.
cit: https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/F3/390/276/506642/ (https://web.archive.org/web/20251212035153/https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/F3/390/276/506642/)

Because this does not offer aditional metadata or documentation it is not subject to copyright/DMCA.
